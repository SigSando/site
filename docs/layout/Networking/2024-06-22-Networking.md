##  <span style="color: royalblue; font-weight: bold;">Why is a Network imporant??</span>
- A network is something to consider about when you join any type of Wi-Fi or school/library computer. In the digital world we live in today, a network can be defined by one or more devices connected to the same group of devices that share an internet connection. Its important to know sharing a connection is a lot more significant than the words: "free internet" or "Free Wi-Fi".

- Most importantly Networks may allow sharing of much more than you anticpate.
- Secondly there are likely to be policies or lack OF policies that you should be aware of.



##  <span style="color: royalblue; font-weight: bold;">What is a "Network" really?</span>


- A `network` is a group of interconnected devices (all on the same Wi-Fi network or Networks (can be multiple lines)) that can exchange data and resources together. Most notably, they share an internet connection. A simple home network with common household items such as **smartphones**, **laptops**, **printers**, **smart devices**, **TV's** and **other devices** on a home wifi is considered a network.

- Also, A workplace has many networks. They may have a network that only deals with providing PXE boot images for wiping old reusable laptops, new laptops and PCs.


- They can also have separate networks for allowing remote access over a VPN
- Often workplaces provide different profiles for VPN access. this could be for temporary use, regular office workplace 


## Networking continued
